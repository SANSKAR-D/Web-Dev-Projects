<h3>Problem Statement</h3><p>You are tasked with creating a "Love Calculator" to determine the love percentage between two people based on their names.

​The love percentage is calculated using the following rules:

​Take both names and combine them into a single string.
​Count the total number of times the letters in the word "TRUE" ('t', 'r', 'u', 'e') appear in the combined string. This will be the first digit(s) of the percentage.

​Count the total number of times the letters in the word "LOVE" ('l', 'o', 'v', 'e') appear in the combined string. This will be the second digit(s) of the percentage.

​Concatenate these two counts to form an integer. For example, if the "TRUE" count is 5 and the "LOVE" count is 3, the percentage is 53%.

​If the resulting concatenated number is greater than 100, cap the final percentage at 100%.</p><h3>Input</h3><p>​A single line containing two space-separated strings, name1 and name2.</p><h3>Output</h3><p>​Print a single integer representing the calculated love percentage.</p><h3>Example</h3><p>Example-1<br><br>​Input: Angela Dwight<br>​Output: 12<br><br>​Explanation: * "TRUE" letters in "AngelaDwight": 't' (1), 'e' (0), 'r' (0), 'u' (0) -> Count = 1<br>​"LOVE" letters in "AngelaDwight": 'l' (1), 'e' (1), 'o' (0), 'v' (0) -> Count = 2<br><br>​Resulting percentage: 12%</p>
